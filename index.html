English, which was awarded Williamstown Theatre Festival’s 2020 L. Arnold Weissberger New Play Award, was also awarded the 2023 Pulitzer Prize for Drama.

    WE’VE HAD CULTURAL AND ECONOMIC IMPACT ON OUR LOCAL COMMUNITY

    From our inception, we’ve been part of the fabric of the economy of the Berkshires. In addition to the tourism our programming has brought to the community, we have a history of employing local staff and artists, providing income to local businesses involved in supporting our festival, and generating revenue from ticket sales and sponsorships.

As recently as July 2023, a critic from the NY Times wrote: “Over a couple of days in Western Massachusetts last week, I saw two plays, one play reading and one cabaret, and if you looked at the quality of what was there — rather than the quantity of what was not — you’d hardly know that anything was amiss.”

  4. How we’re meeting this moment









    OUR STORY OF THE FUTURE

    What has happened for 70 summers in Williamstown is hard to describe to those who have not been. It’s a coming together of people and place, talent and town, star power and service to the craft. It is alchemy.

    But, the American theatre is in peril. The pandemic decimated our audiences. A global racial reckoning surfaced our industry’s exclusivity and bias. A national labor reckoning challenged our way of working. Funding, as part of a sector-wide drought, is scarce. And the American theatre doesn’t hold the same cultural relevance and impact as it did 70 years ago.

    As our screens distance us further from a tangible reality, gathering becomes an increasingly radical act. Theatre is gathering—a shared communal experience that sparks understanding, connection, joy, and discovery. And so, theatre can heal, help us cope, and help us live longer, better lives. Theatre creates the conditions for personal and societal transformation.

    In a rapidly changing world, our industry is being called to respond to our challenges with innovation, breaking new ground, and inventing different ways forward. Pushing our time-tested form to tackle current realities of our society: widespread isolation, disconnection from nature, ideological division and social disparity. A better future depends on it.

    Williamstown Theatre Festival is a place of connection for people—to one another, to nature, to difference, to diversity, to culture, to theatre.

    We craft transportive experiences for audiences. We cultivate new ways of working and learning for artists. We nurture the local community that nurtures us, lifting the region of the Berkshires, its environment and its local economy. We build bridges of access to the American theatre through our platforms. We shape generations of theatre-makers and theatre-goers.

    Williamstown Theatre Festival brings us together and transforms us. Because there’s nothing else like it when WTF is happening.

    HOW WE’LL INVEST

    2024

    Design our foundation

    What success looks like…

    • Our internal and external systems for the next iteration of our business are fully designed and readied for implementation
    • We’ve refreshed our brand, marketing, and communications and have begun to signal our transformation with internal and external stakeholders
    • We have the funding required to jumpstart the implementation of our vision for the next 3-5 years
    2025

    Build and test a prototype

    What success looks like…

    • Our business function is more aligned with our vision, and we have clear plans for refinement and growth
    • We’ve produced both year-round and seasonal programming that fully reflects our vision
    • We’ve begun to build revenue streams of earned income, further identifying priorities for growth
    2026

    Iterate our prototype

    What success looks like…

    • We’ve refined our business function and have begun to create resilience and stability
    • We’ve produced another successful wave of year-round and seasonal programming that fully reflects our vision
    • We’ve focused on the strategic priorities that have allowed us to grow our revenue and our business
    2027

    Build our work

    What success looks like…

    • Our business function is fully optimized for our ambition
    • We’re planning and producing year-round and seasonal programming that reflects our vision and is meeting our ambition to build new audiences, connect with existing audiences, and engage with and influence culture
    • We’ve achieved growth across our key priorities and are moving toward a self-sustainable model
    2028

    Scale our work

    What success looks like…

    • We have a fully functioning organization, and are growing with our business
    • Our programming continues to meet our ambitions, growing audiences and increasing engagement
    • We’ve continued growth across our key priorities and are closer to a self-sustained model
    • We’ve established key partnerships that scale our impact
