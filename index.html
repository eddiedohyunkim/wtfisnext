    WE’VE MADE SIGNIFICANT CONTRIBUTIONS TO THE LANDSCAPE OF THE AMERICAN THEATER:

    Williamstown Theatre Festival has a history of developmental work and full productions continuing life on Broadway, Off-Broadway and regionally. This season alone, Williamstown Theatre Festival is represented on Broadway by both Here Lies Love and Lempicka. Other Broadway, Off-Broadway, and regional representation has included The Elephant Man, The Bridges of Madison County, The Sound Inside, The Visit, Fool for Love, Choir Boy, Paradise Blue, Grand Horizons, The Rose Tattoo, Seared, Selling Kabul, Unknown Soldier, One Mo' Time, Hedda Gabler, The Man Who Had All the Luck, and See What I Wanna See, among others.

    Cost of Living, which was developed and premiered at Williamstown Theatre Festival, was awarded the 2018 Pulitzer Prize for Drama and made its debut on Broadway in Fall 2022. English, which was awarded Williamstown Theatre Festival’s 2020 L. Arnold Weissberger New Play Award, was also awarded the 2023 Pulitzer Prize for Drama.

  3. How we’re meeting this moment









    OUR STORY OF THE FUTURE

    What has happened for 70 summers in Williamstown is hard to describe to those who have not been. It’s a coming together of people and place, talent and town, star power and service to the craft. It is alchemy.

    But, the American theatre is in peril. The pandemic decimated our audiences. A global racial reckoning surfaced our industry’s exclusivity and bias. A national labor reckoning challenged our way of working. Funding, as part of a sector-wide drought, is scarce. And the American theatre doesn’t hold the same cultural relevance and impact as it did 70 years ago.

    As our screens distance us further from a tangible reality, gathering becomes an increasingly radical act. Theatre is gathering—a shared communal experience that sparks understanding, connection, joy, and discovery. And so, theatre can heal, help us cope, and help us live longer, better lives. Theatre creates the conditions for personal and societal transformation.

    In a rapidly changing world, our industry is being called to respond to our challenges with innovation, breaking new ground, and inventing different ways forward. Pushing our time-tested form to tackle current realities of our society: widespread isolation, disconnection from nature, ideological division and social disparity. A better future depends on it.

    Williamstown Theatre Festival is a place of connection for people—to one another, to nature, to difference, to diversity, to culture, to theatre.

    We craft transportive experiences for audiences. We cultivate new ways of working and learning for artists. We nurture the local community that nurtures us, lifting the region of the Berkshires, its environment and its local economy. We build bridges of access to the American theatre through our platforms. We shape generations of theatre-makers and theatre-goers.

    Williamstown Theatre Festival brings us together and transforms us. Because there’s nothing else like it when WTF is happening.
